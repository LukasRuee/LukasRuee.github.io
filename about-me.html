About Me

I really enjoy createing clean and high quality projects

One of the aspects I love most about game development is the opportunity to blend creativity with technical problem-solving. I thrive on turning innovative ideas into reality by coding complex game mechanics, developing dynamic and intelligent AI behaviors and refining the player experience. I’m constantly learning new techniques and technologies to improve the quality and performance of my games.

Previous Experience: From Electronics Engineer to Game Developer

Before pursuing game development and coding in general, I worked as an Electronics Engineer. This experience has given me a solid understanding of hardware systems, circuit design, and troubleshooting electronic components. I’ve worked extensively with embedded systems and microcontrollers, which has not only enhanced my problem-solving skills but also provided me with a deep understanding of how software interfaces with hardware. I started by programming in Assembler, then C all the way up to C# and more high-level languages.

My engineering background has given me a unique perspective on software development, especially when it comes to optimizing performance and troubleshooting technical issues. I approach coding with an engineer’s precision, ensuring that my code is efficient, scalable, and easy to maintain. This mindset has proven invaluable when working with resource-constrained environments, whether it's optimizing a game for different platforms or squeezing every bit of performance out of a system.

My Journey as a Game Developer

My journey in game development started as a hobby while I was still working as an electronics engineer. I started experimenting with simple 2D game engines and quickly fell in love with the creative process. From there, I transitioned into learning more complex game engines like Unity and Unreal Engine. Over the years, I’ve been refining my skills by working on a wide range of projects — everything from Tetris on a ST microcontroller with an simple monochrom-display up to entries to larger, more complex game prototypes and game jams.

One of my proudest achievements is a 3D adventure game prototype I developed, where I focused on creating realistic AI behaviors and a highly interactive game environment. This project not only sharpened my programming skills but also taught me a lot about complex systems, unmanaged code structures, and more. As I continue my education at SAE Institute, I’m eager to take on more ambitious projects and push the boundaries of what I can create.
